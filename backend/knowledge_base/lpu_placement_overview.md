# LPU Placement Overview — Academic Year 2023-24

## About LPU Placement Cell

Lovely Professional University (LPU) maintains one of the most active placement cells among private universities in India. The **Division of Career Services (DCS)** manages end-to-end placement activities — from corporate tie-ups and pre-placement training to offer letters and onboarding support.

The placement cell operates year-round and has dedicated placement officers for each department. Students receive continuous support through:
- Industry-aligned training programs (aptitude, soft skills, technical)
- Mock interview sessions
- Resume building workshops
- Campus placement drives
- Off-campus referrals and job fair participation

**Official Portal:** [myplacements.lpu.in](https://myplacements.lpu.in)
**Email:** placements@lpu.co.in
**Helpdesk:** Placement cell, Block 32, LPU campus, Jalandhar, Punjab

## Eligibility Criteria (General)

> **Note:** Each company sets its own criteria. These are the general LPU placement registration requirements.

### Minimum Academic Requirements
- **CGPA:** 6.0 or above (on a 10-point scale)
- **10th Standard:** 60% or above
- **12th Standard:** 60% or above
- **Active Backlogs:** Nil (no pending backlogs at time of placement)
- **Year Gap:** Typically ≤1 year (company-dependent)

### Additional Notes
- Students must be in their final year of study (8th semester / last semester)
- Pre-final year students can sit for internship placements
- Students who have already received an offer may be restricted from certain drives (depends on CTC band)
- CGPA freeze is mandatory before placement registration

## Placement Timeline

LPU follows a structured placement calendar aligned with industry hiring cycles:

### Phase 1 — IT / Software Companies (August – November)
- **August–September:** Mass recruiters (TCS, Infosys, Wipro, Cognizant) begin NQT / OA
- **September–October:** Mid-tier IT companies (HCL, Mphasis, L&T Infotech, Mindtree)
- **October–November:** Top-tier companies (Amazon, Microsoft, Atlassian, Samsung R&D)

> This is the most competitive phase. Most CSE/IT students get placed here.

### Phase 2 — Core Engineering & Analytics (December – February)
- Manufacturing, automotive, FMCG companies visit
- Analytics companies: MuSigma, Fractal, ZS Associates
- Government PSUs (separate process — GATE required for most)

### Phase 3 — Remaining Offers & Late Drives (March – May)
- Startups, consulting firms
- Off-campus placements and job fairs
- PPO conversions from internships

## Key Preparation Areas

### 1. Data Structures & Algorithms (DSA)
The single most important area for IT placements. Covers:
- Arrays, Strings, Linked Lists, Stacks, Queues
- Trees, Graphs, Heaps, Tries
- Dynamic Programming, Greedy, Divide & Conquer
- Sorting, Searching, Binary Search

**Recommended:** LeetCode (NeetCode 150 list), InterviewBit, GeeksforGeeks

### 2. Core Computer Science Subjects
- **DBMS:** SQL queries, normalization, transactions, indexes
- **OS:** Scheduling, memory management, deadlocks, synchronization
- **Computer Networks:** OSI model, TCP/IP, DNS, HTTP, routing
- **OOP:** Four pillars, SOLID principles, design patterns

### 3. Aptitude & Reasoning
- Quantitative Aptitude: Percentages, Time-Speed-Distance, Profit/Loss, Ratios
- Logical Reasoning: Series, blood relations, syllogisms, seating arrangement
- Verbal Ability: Reading comprehension, grammar, vocabulary

**Recommended:** IndiaBix, PrepInsta, RS Aggarwal

### 4. Communication & Behavioral Skills
- Resume writing (ATS-friendly format)
- HR interview preparation (Tell me about yourself, STAR format)
- Group discussion techniques
- Technical presentation skills

### 5. Domain-Specific Skills
- For Data Science roles: Python, ML basics, Statistics, Pandas/NumPy
- For Cloud roles: AWS/Azure fundamentals
- For Embedded/ECE roles: C/C++, microcontrollers, communication protocols

## Placement Registration Process

1. **Register on myplacements.lpu.in** — Upload CGPA, resume, documents
2. **Profile verification** by placement cell (3-5 working days)
3. **Shortlisting** — Companies share eligibility criteria, shortlisting happens from portal
4. **Notification** — Email + SMS + portal notification before each drive
5. **Drive participation** — Online/On-campus test, interviews
6. **Offer release** — Formal offer letter via portal after selection
7. **Joining** — Coordinate directly with company HR

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I sit for multiple companies simultaneously?**
A: Yes, until you receive an offer above a certain CTC threshold set by the placement cell. After receiving an offer >X LPA (varies by batch), you may be restricted from some drives.

**Q: What if I have a backlog?**
A: Students with active backlogs at the time of registration are generally not eligible. Clear all backlogs before your 7th semester.

**Q: Is CGPA the only criterion?**
A: No. Companies also look at projects, internships, coding skills, communication, and leadership roles.

**Q: Are off-campus placements supported?**
A: The placement cell encourages off-campus applications. You can use placement cell referrals and alumni networks.

**Q: How are PPOs handled?**
A: Pre-Placement Offers from internships are directly processed through the placement cell with company coordination.
